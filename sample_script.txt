Q: Please introduce yourself.
A: Certainly. My name is Lee, and I have worked in marine engineering for more than twenty years. I have experience on bulk carriers, container vessels, VLCCs, chemical tankers, product tankers, and LNG carriers. In recent years, I have mainly worked on LNG vessels, and I have handled modern engine types including X-DF and ME-GA. As Chief Engineer, I always focus on safety, reliability, proper planning, and good teamwork between engine room staff and other departments.

Q: Why do you want to join Seapeak?
A: I am interested in joining Seapeak because the company has a strong reputation in LNG shipping, safety culture, and professional standards. At this stage of my career, I want to work in an environment where safe operation, technical discipline, and long-term performance are taken seriously. I believe my LNG experience and my practical engine room leadership can contribute positively to the company.

Q: How do you manage an engine room team?
A: My style is simple and practical. First, I make sure the team understands the work clearly, including the risk, the method, and the expected standard. Second, I try to assign the right person to the right job. Third, I always follow up, because instruction alone is not enough. I believe a Chief Engineer should be calm, consistent, and visible. When the team trusts the system and the leadership, the engine room works much better.
